\noindent\textbf{Goal.}
This note completes the proof of \Cref{thm:GCFT_reduced} (``Theorem 2'').
The two-case argument of the thesis reduces everything, in the ramified case
$\mdls > 0$, to the following extension lemma, whose proof in the thesis is
currently a placeholder (\texttt{ProofOfReducedTheorem.tex}): a locally
constant sheaf $\cF^{[d]}$ on $U^{(d)}$ which is \emph{tamely} ramified along
the boundary divisor $H = \Cmod{d} \setminus U^{(d)}$ extends to a locally
constant sheaf on all of $\Cmod{d}$.

The proof below proceeds in two independent steps: by Zariski--Nagata purity,
it suffices to show the corresponding torsor is \emph{unramified} at the
generic point $\eta_0$ of $H$ (\Cref{prop:extension_via_purity}); then
tameness at $\eta_0$ is upgraded to unramifiedness
(\Cref{prop:tame_at_boundary_implies_unramified}) --- the wild part is killed
by tameness alone, and the tame part by a Kummer-theoretic valuation
computation using the projective bundle structure of $C^{(d)}$ over
$\PicC[d]$.

All numbered references to results \emph{not} proved in this note
(Takeuchi's compactification, the degree condition, purity inputs, etc.)
carry the numbering of the compiled thesis (\texttt{main.pdf}).
