\title{Geometric Class Field Theory}
\author{Assaf Marzan}
\date{\today}
\maketitle
\begin{abstract}
We calculate the ramification of the symmetric product $\cF^{[\deg \mdls]}$ of a local system $\cF$ on a curve $C \setminus \mdls$. 
Assuming the ramification of $\cF$ is bounded by $\mdls$, we find that the symmetric product $\cF^{[\deg \mdls]}$ is at most tamely ramified at the generic point of 
the exceptional divisor $E_\mdls$ of the blowup of $C^{(\deg \mdls)}$ at $\mdls$. As a primary application, 
we utilize this result to prove Geometric Class Field Theory. Our approach builds upon the geometric framework for the unramified case originally established by Deligne for the rank-one Langlands correspondence, 
following the subsequent extensions to the ramified case developed by Takeuchi and Guignard.
\end{abstract}
